FAQ

Erasmus+

Who can take part?

The youngest people can be 13 years old and the oldest 30 years old. Usually, the organizers set criteria regarding the age of the participants. Each group must have an adult group leader

How long does a youth exchange take?

The exchange can take from 5 to 21 days (not including travel days)

How many people count the youth exchange?

from 16 to even 60 people can take part in it

How to go on a youth exchange?

If you are not related to an organization (youth, non-governmental, student, informal group, association or sports club) that implements Erasmus + projects, you need to look for one, and if you are on the website of our Foundation, you have come to the right place, we will be happy to help you

Youthpass, what is it?

At the end of the exchange, you have the option of assessing yourself with the Youthpass certificate. This is a summary of information about the knowledge you acquired during the project. The certificate consists of 8 key competences:

1. Communication in the mother tongue
2. Communication in foreign languages
3. Mathematical competences and basic scientific and technical competences
4. IT competences
5. Learning to learn
6. Social and civic competences
7. Initiative and entrepreneurship
8. Cultural awareness and expression

Why is it worth taking part in the exchange? 

There are plenty of reasons why it is worth it. First of all, it is a great opportunity to get to know other cultures, great young people, travel and broaden your worldview, as well as improve your language skills. You will surely remember participating in projects for many years to come
