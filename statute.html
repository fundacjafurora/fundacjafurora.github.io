STATUTE



FURORA FOUNDATION
based in Warsaw


CHAPTER I.
General provisions


§ 1.

1. The Foundation under the name Furora, hereinafter referred to as the Foundation, is established by Natalia Brodzik and Marcin Tempczyk - hereinafter referred to as the Founders, by a notarial deed drawn up on June 20, 2021 in front of Łukasz Kociszewski, notary public at the Notary's Office in Warsaw, at ul. Nowowiejska 4, under the repertory number A 733/2021,

2. The Foundation operates on the basis of the provisions of Polish law, in particular the Act of April 6, 1984 on foundations (i.e. Journal of Laws of 1991, No. 46, item 203, as amended) and the provisions of this statute,

3. The Foundation has legal personality,

4. The Foundation is established for an indefinite period.



§ 2.

1. The seat of the Foundation is the city of Warsaw,

2. The area of ​​the Foundation's activity is the territory of the Republic of Poland. The foundation can lead
activity outside the territory of the Republic of Poland,

3. The Foundation may establish branches, branches and representative offices in this country and abroad,

4. The Foundation may join companies, foundations, associations and social formations,

5. For the purposes of international cooperation, the Foundation may use a name translation
in selected foreign languages.




§ 3.

1. The Foundation is supervised by the Minister of Education,

2. The Foundation uses symbols and seals according to the patterns established by the Foundation's Management Board,

3. The Foundation may award medals, honorary titles, diplomas and other distinctions to natural and legal persons of merit for the Foundation and contributing to the achievement of the Foundation's goals.




CHAPTER II
Goals and principles of the foundation's operation


§ 4.

The purpose of the statutory activity of the Foundation is:

• Initiation and implementation of activities for the development, education and improvement of young people's competences,
• Equal opportunities for the development of various social groups and supporting people at risk of exclusion from society,
• Activities for international cooperation aimed at raising public awareness, breaking down barriers and combating negative stereotypes.
• Support and promotion of entrepreneurship development and carrying out activities aimed at sustainable economic growth,
• Promoting and developing attitudes and activities raising civic awareness and encouraging active participation in the life of the society.


§ 5.

The Foundation achieves its goals by:

• Implementation of projects, meetings and international events to spread horizons and European integration,
• Supporting the process of education and development of young people,
• Promoting activities to eliminate barriers to the education of people with fewer opportunities,
• Supporting activities aimed at counteracting the social exclusion of children and adolescents,
• Organizing and financing scientific meetings, trainings and courses to educate people interested in implementing the statutory goals of the Foundation,
• Conducting charity activities,
• Promotion and organization of volunteering,
• Conducting activities for the integration of various environments and development of cooperation and exchange of experiences between young people from different countries,
• activities supporting economic development, including the development of entrepreneurship,
• Organizing sports, recreational and cultural events,
• undertaking cooperation with individuals and organizations, both Polish and foreign, pursuing goals similar to the goals of the Foundation,
• providing organizational, information, technical and financial support to individuals and entities in connection with the implementation of the Foundation's goals,
• other activities aimed at achieving the Foundation's goals.



CHAPTER III
Property and income of the Foundation

§ 6.


The assets of the Foundation are the Founding Fund in the amount of PLN 1,000 (one thousand) and other property such as: financial resources, real estate, movables acquired by the Foundation in the course of its operation.


§ 7.

The Foundation's income may come in particular from:

• domestic and foreign donations, inheritances, bequests,
• subsidies, donations, subsidies and grants,
• income from collections and public events,
• income from the Foundation's movable and immovable property,
• paid activities of a public benefit organization,
• interest,
• interest on capital investments and securities,
• payments of 1% of tax from taxpayers,
• other influences not prohibited by law.


§ 8.

Income from grants, subsidies, donations, inheritances, bequests and other sources may be used for any of the Foundation's goals, unless the testator or donor has specified a specific purpose.



§ 9.

• The Foundation may raise funds in the Polish currency, as well as in foreign currencies,
at relevant banks, in accordance with the provisions of Polish law,

• the foundation may invest its assets,

• The property is earmarked for the implementation of statutory objectives.



CHAPTER IV
Foundation authorities

§ 10.

The authority of the Foundation is the Management Board of the Foundation, hereinafter referred to as the Management Board.


Foundation Board

§ 11.

• Under the Articles of Association, the Management Board may include the Founders,
• Founders may act as the President of the Management Board and the Vice-President of the Management Board, In the event of the Founders resigning from the function of a member of the Management Board, they make a decision on the selection of Members of the Management Board,
• The Management Board of the Foundation consists of 2 to 6 people appointed for a five-year term,
• Members of the Management Board are appointed and dismissed by the Founders. If the Founders are unable to appoint or dismiss members of the Management Board due to random reasons, the person authorized to appoint or dismiss members of the Management Board is the President of the Management Board. If one of the Founders is also the President of the Management Board and cannot appoint or dismiss members of the Management Board for random reasons, these rights are taken over by the Vice President of the Management Board.
• The function of a member of the Management Board may be performed for any number of terms,
• Membership in the Management Board ceases on:
• expiry of the term of office,
• written resignation,
• death of a member of the Management Board,
• In the event of failure to perform the duties of a member of the Management Board, the Founders, at the request of other members of the Management Board, may dismiss a member of the Management Board.



§ 12.

• The Management Board manages the activities of the Foundation and represents the Foundation outside,

• The competences of the Management Board include all matters not stipulated in this Statute
for founders,

• Members of the Management Board may receive remuneration,

• Members of the Management Board may not be convicted by a final judgment for an intentional crime prosecuted by public indictment or a tax offense,

• The Management Board may appoint consultants to evaluate the activities undertaken by the Foundation
and their teams and commission them to prepare appropriate studies.



§ 13.

The tasks of the Management Board include in particular:

1) adopting annual financial plans,
2) managing the assets of the Foundation,
3) preparing reports on the activities of the Foundation,
4) managing the day-to-day activities of the Foundation,
5) implementation of the statutory goals of the Foundation,
6) accepting donations, inheritance, bequests as well as grants and subsidies,
7) determining the size of employment, the amount of remuneration, funds for remuneration
and awards for employees of the Foundation,
8) making decisions in all matters related to the activities of the Foundation,
9) establishing and closing branches of the Foundation,
10) making decisions on joining companies or other organizations, and
about their creation,
11) adopting regulations,
12) making decisions to amend the Foundation's statute,
13) making decisions in all other matters not reserved to the competence of other authorities,
14) approval of financial statements.



§ 14.

• Meetings of the Management Board are held as needed, but not less frequently than once a quarter,

• Meetings of the Management Board are convened by the President of the Management Board, by sending information about the date by e-mail, and if this is not possible by registered mail, at least
3 days before the scheduled meeting,

• The meetings of the Management Board are chaired by the President,

• All members of the Management Board must be informed about the meeting,

• The Management Board adopts resolutions by a simple majority of votes of the Management Board members present
at the meeting,

• In the event of a tied vote, the President has the casting vote.



The method of representation and incurring property liabilities

§ 15.
Each member of the Management Board is authorized individually to incur obligations and submit declarations of will on behalf of the Foundation.




CHAPTER VI
Liquidation of the Foundation

§ 16.

• The Foundation is liquidated if the goals for which it was established are achieved
or in the event of exhaustion of funds and assets of the Foundation,

• The decision on the liquidation of the Foundation is made by the Foundation's Management Board,

• Liquidators are appointed and dismissed by the Foundation's Management Board,

• The funds and property remaining after the liquidation of the Foundation may be allocated, by virtue of a resolution of the Management Board, to foundations with similar goals operating in the Republic of Poland.





CHAPTER VII
Final Provisions

§ 17.
• For the effective implementation of its goals, the Foundation may merge with another foundation,

• The decision to merge is made by the Foundation's Management Board,

• A merger with another foundation may not take place if it could significantly change the Foundation's goal.


§ 18.

The statute comes into force on the day it is registered by the National Court Register.
